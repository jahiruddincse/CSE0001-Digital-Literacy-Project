## Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

### What is UPI Payment Fraud?
UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime where users are tricked into sending money instead of receiving it. It mainly occurs due to lack of awareness about how digital payment systems work.

### Realistic Scenario
A first-year college student named Aman posted a laptop for sale online. A fraudster contacted him pretending to be a buyer and sent a payment request, saying Aman must approve it to receive money. Without understanding, Aman entered his UPI PIN, and ₹5000 was deducted from his account.

### How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. Fraudster contacts victim through social media or marketplaces  
2. Builds trust by acting as a buyer or official  
3. Sends a payment request or QR code  
4. Creates urgency (“payment will expire”)  
5. Victim enters UPI PIN  
6. Money gets deducted instead of received  

### Who is Targeted?
Students, online sellers, and frequent UPI users are most targeted due to high usage and limited awareness.

### Consequences
Victims suffer financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering money. Many realize the fraud only after the transaction is completed.

### Conclusion
This case shows that cybercrime often exploits human mistakes rather than system flaws. Awareness and careful verification are essential to stay safe.
